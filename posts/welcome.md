Most AI commentary is written by people who don't ship anything. I build machine
learning systems for a telecom subscriber base of over 70 million people — churn
models, segmentation, next-best-offer engines. The gap between what gets hyped and
what survives contact with production is enormous, and that gap is what I want to
write about.

## What lives here

**A weekday digest.** Every morning, Monday to Friday, a bot I built pulls the day's
AI and tech stories, filters out the noise, and summarises what's left. Each item
links to its original source — this is a pointer to the news, not a replacement for it.

**Paper picks.** Fresh arXiv work in machine learning, filtered to the things I
actually care about: retrieval, LLMs, churn, segmentation, tabular data.

**My own writing and research.** Longer pieces — what worked, what broke, and what
the metrics said afterwards. Less "here's what's possible", more "here's what happened
when I tried it on real data at scale."

## The bias I'm bringing

I care about the boring parts. Feature pipelines that don't silently rot. Models whose
lift survives a quarter in production. Segmentation you can actually hand to a marketing
team without a translation layer. If a technique can't clear that bar, I'm not very
interested in how impressive its benchmark looks.

If that's useful to you, the digest lands every weekday. Subscribe or grab the
[RSS feed](feed.xml) — no algorithm in between.
